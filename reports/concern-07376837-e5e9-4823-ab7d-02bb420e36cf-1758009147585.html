Strategy 1: Structured Movement Breaks & Non-Verbal Cue System


  • Research Base: PBIS; research on incorporating movement to improve focus in students with ADHD (e.g., Rapport et al., 2008).

  • Materials Needed: A "Break Pass" (a laminated card with his name on it); a pre-arranged signal (e.g., a colored sticky note on his desk from the teacher); a timer; a designated "break zone" in the classroom (e.g., a quiet corner to stand and stretch, a short walk to the water fountain and back).

  • Implementation Steps:

1. Day 1-2 (You & Teacher): Meet with Noel. Say: *"Noel, we want to help you succeed. Your brain learns better when it gets a quick move. We're going to try a system. When you feel restless, you can show your teacher this Break Pass. She might also give you this signal [demonstrate] to let you know it's a good time for a 2-minute movement break. This isn't a punishment; it's a tool."*
2. Day 3-14 (Teacher implements, you monitor): Teacher integrates this. *Before* a long period of seated work, the teacher proactively says, "Noel, your break time is in 10 minutes. Let's see how much you can get done before then." The timer is set for 2 minutes. This is predictable and non-disruptive.
  • Data Collection: Teacher tracks frequency of break use and a simple +/- for whether he returns to task successfully.

  • Success Criteria: Noel uses the break appropriately 80% of the time without escalating behavior; he returns to task and engages for at least a 5-minute period post-break.

Strategy 2: Chunking & Visual Work Organizer


  • Research Base: Executive Function skill scaffolding (Dawson & Guare, 2018); UDL Checkpoint 6.1 (Guide appropriate goal-setting).

  • Materials Needed: A pocket folder; sticky notes; a "Work Map" graphic organizer that breaks assignments into 3-4 clear steps.

  • Implementation Steps:

1. Preparation (You & Teacher): The teacher pre-chunks any worksheet or assignment into smaller parts (e.g., "Part A: Questions 1-3," "Part B: Chart").
2. Implementation (Teacher): When assigning work, the teacher gives Noel his "Work Map" and a sticky note for each chunk. As he finishes each chunk, he moves the sticky note to the "Done" side. This provides constant visual feedback and a sense of accomplishment.
  • Data Collection: Percentage of chunked assignments completed per class period.

  • Success Criteria: Increase from baseline in the percentage of assigned work chunks completed within the class period.

4. Short-Term Intensive Support (Weeks 3-8)

Primary Focus Area: Self-Monitoring of On-Task Behavior


  • Intervention Program: Simple Self-Monitoring Checklist.

  • Frequency: 3 times per week, during one challenging academic period.
