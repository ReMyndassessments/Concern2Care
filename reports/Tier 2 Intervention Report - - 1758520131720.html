#### Instructional Approaches (Universal Design for Learning - UDL)

  • Consistent Visual Schedules: Post a clear, visual schedule for the day. Review it each morning and especially before transitions (like the pre-lunch period). This reduces anxiety for students like Sonia and Crystal who struggle with unpredictability.

  • Chunk & Process Time:

  • "Cafeteria Countdown": For the noisy pre-lunch period, implement a highly structured, chunked activity. Use a timer for a 10-minute silent reading block, followed by a 5-minute "pair and share" to discuss what they read. The structure and short timeframes override the external distraction.

  • Think-Pair-Share: For all instruction. This gives processing time for students like Josie who blurt out answers and allows anxious students (Crystal) to test ideas with one peer before speaking to the whole class.

  • Differentiated Output: Offer choices for how students demonstrate understanding (e.g., write a paragraph, create a graphic organizer, record a voice memo, draw a comic strip). This supports Noel, Jay, and gifted learners simultaneously.

#### Behavioral Support Systems (Positive Behavioral Interventions & Supports - PBIS)

  • Clear, Positively Stated Expectations: Co-create 3-5 whole-class expectations with your students (e.g., "We are safe. We are respectful. We are learners."). Teach what each looks and sounds like in different settings.

  • Group Contingency Systems:

  • "Focus Frenzy": During the challenging pre-lunch period, use a whole-class reward system. Place 5 marbles in a jar at the start of the lesson. For every 5 minutes the whole class is on task despite the noise, you add a marble. If off-task behavior occurs, you simply *pause* without removing marbles. If they reach a set goal by lunch, the class earns a reward (e.g., 5 minutes of extra recess, a dance party Friday).

  • Randomized Recognition: Use a wheel of names or popsicle sticks to randomly call on students to "catch them being good" and reward behavior that meets expectations. This ensures all students, not just the loudest, get attention.
