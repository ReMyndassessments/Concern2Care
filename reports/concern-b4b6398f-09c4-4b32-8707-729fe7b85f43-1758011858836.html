Strategy 1: Non-Verbal Check-In & Check-Out (CICO) System


  • Research Base: Hawken et al., (2007); Crone, Horner, & Hawken (2004). A cornerstone of Tier 2 PBIS, highly effective for students needing increased positive adult attention and clear behavioral expectations.

  • Materials Needed: A simple, single-page daily report card (DRC); a designated spot in each classroom (e.g., a folder on the wall by the door).

  • Implementation Steps:

1. Morning (8:05 AM, 2 mins): You meet Noel at his first-period class. Greet him positively. Briefly review the day's schedule (using his planner). Give him his DRC. The goal is connection, not correction.
2. Each Period (Transition Time, 30 secs): Noel brings his DRC to the teacher. The teacher gives a quick rating (e.g., 2=Met Expectation, 1=Needs Reminder, 0=Did Not Meet) on 2-3 target behaviors (e.g., "Started work promptly," "Respected others").
3. End of Day (2:55 PM, 5 mins): Noel checks out with you. You quickly total the points, provide specific praise for successes, and problem-solve *one* small thing for tomorrow. This data is your daily fuel.
  • Data Collection: The DRC itself is the primary data tool. Track daily point totals and trends in specific periods/teachers.

  • Success Criteria: 80% or higher of possible daily points for 3 consecutive days; Noel initiating the check-in/out process.

Strategy 2: "Focus Folder" for Academic Task Management


  • Research Base: Executive Function theory (Dawson & Guare, 2018); Universal Design for Learning (UDL) Guideline 6: Provide options for executive functions.

  • Materials Needed: A bright, distinctive two-pocket folder; a stack of "Now & Next" cards; a timer (visual timers are excellent).

  • Implementation Steps:

1. Preparation (Teacher): Work with his teachers to break down assignments onto the "Now & Next" cards. "Now: Problems 1-5. Next: Problems 6-10."
2. In-Class (Student): The folder is on Noel's desk. He works from the "Now" task. When done, he moves the card and starts the "Next." The visual chunking reduces overwhelm.
3. Your Role: Coach him on using the system and check the folder during your CICO meetings to reinforce its use.
  • Data Collection: Teacher anecdotal notes on task initiation and completion; number of assignments turned in.

  • Success Criteria: Increase in completed classwork; reduction in student-reported feelings of being "lost" or "overwhelmed."
